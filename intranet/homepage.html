What we do

We look after the Mayor of London brand. We make sure all public facing design work has a consistent look and feel.

To highlight how our public facing materials should look we created the Mayor of London brand guidelines. Our editorial guidelines cover the type of language we should use. Together, they give our work an identity and personality. Being consistent means Londoners can recognise our communications and better understand what the Mayor does.

  • Download the brand guidelines.
  • Download the editorial guidelines.

We work closely with the Marketing team producing creative ideas for campaigns. If you're working on a project that requires marketing support (so if you need help reaching your audience or want to look at different ways to do this) you should contact the Marketing team.

If your project doesn't require marketing services, please use this guide which contains very useful information about how we work.

We also provide:

  • copywriting, editing and proofreading services
  • in-house photography services
  • access to the City Hall image library, a large and growing collection of images you can browse, select and download
  • design advice, creative ideas and layout for materials like City Hall exhibitions, reports/brochures, pull up banners etc
  • advice on logo use
  • Mayor of London and London Assembly logo requests
  • templates for MS Word reports, Powerpoint presentations and electronic letterheads.

Photography

If you need photography commissioned, you can use our in house videographer/photographer, Caroline Teo. Please complete the photography briefing form and email it to caroline.teo@london.gov.uk

Where an individual or group are the focus in a photograph you need them to sign a consent form. For individuals or groups under 18 years old please see Child Policy and Protection Procedures

City Hall Image Library

A large and growing collection of images you can browse, select and download. The image library is easy to use, it's search-based, so you can search for images based on the business area you work in.

Within the library you can set up your own collection/s of image selections, you can email them too so someone else can view. There is also a range of standard sizes which each image can be cropped and downloaded.

The images and icons on the site can only be used for Greater London Authority purposes so you can use them on web pages, internal and external publications and documents, Power point slides, email newsletters.

For access please contact your policy comms officer in the first instance, if you don't have one assigned to your team, get in touch.

Royalty free photos are also available upon registration from Transport for London (TfL images), London & Partners (London on View - images of London) and Sport England (sports and activities).

Logos

If you need a Mayor of London or supported by Mayor of London logo please complete and return this email form. All logo use must have project approval from a Mayoral Advisor for Mayor of London or an Assembly Member for London Assembly.

Templates

MS Word reports

For you to use to help format public-facing documents. Get in touch if you would like us to send this to you. We have also put together a short guide which outlines what you need to do before starting your report.

MS Powerpoint presentations

For internal powerpoint presentations, please use the co-branded template. For public facing powerpoint presentations, please use the Mayor of London template. There is also a London Assembly template. For help with how to use these templates, please download our user guide.

Letterheads

For use with GLA letterheaded paper, please use the overprint template. Personalise where shown and save as a template on your PC. Do not put anything into the header or footer as this will overprint on the logo and City Hall address. For emailing letters rather than posting, please use this template.

Name badges

If you need to produce name badges for any City Hall events, please download either the Mayor of London, GLA or MOPAC branded template.

FAQs

Q. Which logo do I need?
A. Use Mayor of London if we own and manage the project. Use 'supported by' Mayor of London logo if we support or fund the project. Use the GLA logo on things like contracts and internal forms. Please get in touch if you're unsure.
 
Q. Who are our print suppliers?
A. Our print supplier is CDS. You should email quote requests to chris.taylor@cds.co.uk. You will then be assigned a contact lead on your print job. For large volume photocopying, see TfL Reprographics.
 
Q. What if the in-house photographer isn't available?
A. If our in-house photographer is unavailable, download TfL's photography roster.
 
Q. How do I get business cards?
A. You should order cards through your business support administrator.
If you're responsible for ordering cards please call our stationery supplier Pureprint on: 01825 768811. They'll set you up on their coreprint system with a username and password. You can then create and order your business cards online.
 
Q. How do I get the Mayor's signature?
A. If you require the Mayor's signature, please contact the Mayor's Correspondence team, as they need to give approval for the signature to be used - email: zoe.newcombe@london.gov.uk.
 
Q. How do I get an ISBN number?
A. If you require an ISBN number for a publication, please email: design@london.gov.uk with the name of the report and its publication date.
